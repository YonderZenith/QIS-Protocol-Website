Why This Model Instead of Open Source?

Open source would let every corporation‚Äîacross healthcare, agriculture, autonomous vehicles, industrial IoT, and dozens of other domains‚Äîdeploy this technology for free. Zero dollars would fund deployment in developing countries. Zero dollars would fund continued protocol development, security updates, or improvements.

This licensing model solves that problem. It's free for anyone helping people or animals without a profit motive. It's paid for corporations generating profit. That commercial revenue funds two things: humanitarian deployments in regions and applications that would never see this technology otherwise, and continued development of the protocol that benefits every user‚Äîincluding those who pay nothing.

The applicability of QIS across domains is so vast that commercial licensing will generate the capital to deploy this where it would never otherwise reach, and keep improving it for everyone.

Open source democratizes code. This model democratizes deployment.
